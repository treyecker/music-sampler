Tokyo Police Club

Tokyo Police Club is an indie-rock band out of Ontario, Canada. The group officially formed in 2005 and consists of Dave Monks (vocalist and bassist), Graham Wright (keyboardist), John Hook (guitarist) and Greg Alsop (drummer). As with many artists at this time, the band found their name through an online name generator. Fun fact: the four attended high school together in Newmarket, Ontario and played in a band called Suburbia, before disbanding.

Since their formation, the band has found success in the indie-rock and alternative scene and recently released a 10-year anniversary edition of their second studio-album Champ. Tokyo Police Club has released five albums (six if you include their debut EP, A Lesson in Crime). Their debut album Elephant Shell, released in 2008, which has been described as emo, dance-punk, helped to solidify their presence as an up-and-coming band.

Since then their style has become more refined and polished, which can be seen most notably in their 2014 album Forcefield. With that said, their 2018 album, Tokyo Police Club, saw a return to a more grungy sound, as did their 2016 album, Melon Collie and the Infinite Radness.

To find out about concert details and any upcoming news on the band visit their website. If you want to hear all the essentials without having to listen through all of their discography, check out their This is Tokyo Police Club playlist on Spotify.
